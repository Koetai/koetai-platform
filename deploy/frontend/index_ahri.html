🐟 Koetai: Dual Vision in Health Research Data

Seeing Above and Below

The koetai (Anableps anableps), or four-eyed fish, possesses a remarkable adaptation: eyes divided horizontally, allowing it to see simultaneously above and below the waterline. As it navigates the murky waters of mangrove estuaries, it watches for predators above while hunting prey below—maintaining constant dual awareness of its complex environment.

Navigating Health Research Data

Similarly, health research data exploration requires dual vision: seeing both the surface patterns in imaging metadata and clinical forms, and the deep structural relationships linking microscopy, cohort data, and biomedical ontologies. Just as the koetai navigates the tangled roots of mangrove forests, researchers navigate the interconnected landscape of OMERO images, REDCap instruments, and linked biomedical knowledge.

About This AHRI Edition

This edition of Koetai SPARQL Explorer is dedicated to the Africa Health Research Institute (AHRI), which works towards the elimination of HIV and TB disease and the optimal health and well-being of under-resourced populations. It provides access to AHRI's linked research data as RDF, including imaging metadata from OMERO, REDCap form schemas, and Sanger microscopy data.

The koetai metaphor reflects how effective health data research requires simultaneous awareness of multiple perspectives: imaging data and clinical context, individual specimens and population cohorts, raw observations and integrated knowledge graphs.

Datasets

  • AHRI REDCap Forms — Form field definitions and structures from the AHRI resource centre
  • OMERO Imaging Metadata — Projects, datasets, images, pixels, and annotations from the AHRI OMERO server
  • Sanger Microscopy — Whole-slide imaging metadata from the Wellcome Sanger Institute
